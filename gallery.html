Njerũ⁩ Njoka

they/them

"My best teacher in life has been my 100yo Kenyan cũcũ/granny, of the time before money, when we loved all the children, before homelessness and ill-health came to our land"

Njerũ grew up in Embu, Kenya -- a food growing community. Their work is inspired by teachings from their 100yo cũcũ/granny in Embu, and their lived experience of migrating and working from Kenya to Turtle Island and the UK.

They are dedicating their energy to the practice and preservation of indigenous and first nations technologies, wisdoms and languages as sources of creative answers to our global collective problems. Their approach to work, community building and organising is based on the Aembu indigenous collaborative practice ĩrĩma, for community care, food and land sovereignty.

Their areas of interest and past works have included cross-cultural collaborations design, creating shared infrastructure for community story telling and problem solving in food growing, engineering, manufacturing, publishing, animation and performing arts.

The Aembu grow their own food on the slopes of Mt. Kirinyaga/Kenya, and are renowned for living long, healthy, communal lives. This motivates Njerũ's belief in the work of Pulse and Pickle to realise healthier sustainable communities through living and growing locally and centring community over profits. They want to connect and collab with others working towards abolition and food sovereignty practices, indigenous culture and land stewardship, land justice-land back. They believe in co-creating home spaces that nurture community bonds through communal practices, sharing knowledge, skill, and resources between their Kenya, Afrikan and international communities.

"We need to heal the land, to heal the people, there is power in food and home to heal the community and the body."

Associated with:
